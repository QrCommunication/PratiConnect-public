# Start a teleconsultation

This tutorial covers the preparation, conduct, and wrap-up of a video consultation with your patient.

**Duration**: 5 minutes

## Prerequisites

- A computer with a working camera and microphone
- A stable internet connection (minimum 2 Mbps, ideally 5 Mbps)
- A patient created with an email address on file
- An up-to-date browser: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge

---

### 1. Schedule the teleconsultation

- Go to **Calendar**.
- Click on an available time slot.
- In the appointment form:
  - Select the patient.
  - Choose the **Teleconsultation** type.
  - Set the duration.
- Click **Create**.

The patient automatically receives an email containing the date, time, link to join the consultation, and technical instructions. A reminder is sent 2 hours before the appointment.

**Result**: the appointment appears in your calendar with a video icon.

---

### 2. Check your equipment

Before the consultation, test your setup.

- Go to **Settings > Teleconsultation**.
- Click **Test my equipment**.
- Allow camera and microphone access in your browser.
- Verify that you can see yourself in the video preview and that the microphone indicator reacts when you speak.

| Element | What to check |
|---------|---------------|
| Camera | Clear image, front-facing light (no backlighting) |
| Microphone | Clear sound, no echo |
| Connection | Minimum 2 Mbps |
| Environment | Quiet, neutral background |

**Result**: all indicators show green on the test page.

---

### 3. Start the video consultation

A few minutes before the scheduled time:

- Open your **Dashboard**.
- Find the teleconsultation appointment.
- Click **Start teleconsultation**.
- Allow camera and microphone access if prompted by the browser.
- Your video appears. The message "Waiting for patient" displays until the patient joins.

The interface has three areas:
- **Video area**: the patient's video in a large view, yours in a small movable thumbnail.
- **Control bar** (bottom): microphone, camera, screen sharing, recording, tools, end call.
- **Side panel** (right): session notes and shareable tools.

**Result**: the patient's video appears as soon as they join the consultation.

---

### 4. Use tools during the consultation

**Write notes**

- In the side panel, select the **Notes** tab.
- Write your observations during the conversation.
- Notes are saved automatically.

**Share your screen**

- Click the **Screen sharing** button in the control bar.
- Choose what to share: entire screen, specific window, or browser tab.
- The patient sees your shared content in real time.
- Click **Stop sharing** when you are done.

**Send a questionnaire live**

- In the **Tools** panel, click **Send a questionnaire**.
- Choose the questionnaire from the list.
- The patient receives a notification in their video window and can fill it out during the consultation.
- Responses appear in real time in your panel.

**Result**: notes, screen sharing, and questionnaires are accessible without leaving the video call.

---

### 5. Record the session

Recording requires the patient's explicit consent.

- Click the **Record** button in the control bar.
- The patient receives a prompt: "The practitioner would like to record this consultation." They can accept or decline.
- If the patient accepts, recording starts. A red "REC" indicator appears.
- To stop, click **Record** again. The file is saved automatically.

After the session, you can transcribe the recording:
- Open the completed session, **Recording** tab.
- Click **Transcribe**.
- The transcription appears after a few minutes with the full text, an automatic summary, and extracted keywords.

Transcription uses credits: 1 credit per minute of recording. The recording is stored on HDS-compliant servers.

**Result**: the recording and its transcription are attached to the session in the patient's record.

---

### 6. End the consultation

- Click the **End** button (red) in the control bar.
- Confirm the end of the consultation. The patient is disconnected.

The wrap-up follows the same process as an in-office session:
- Review and complete your notes.
- Add the services performed.
- Signature is optional for teleconsultations.
- Click **End session**.

**Result**: the session status changes to "Completed".

---

### 7. Troubleshooting

**The patient cannot connect**

- Suggest they use Chrome, check camera/microphone permissions, refresh the page, or try private browsing.

**The connection quality drops**

- Close other applications and move closer to your router.
- Ask the patient to disable their video (audio only) or switch to 4G/5G if Wi-Fi is unstable.
- As a last resort, turn off your own video to preserve audio quality.

**The video freezes**

- Click **Pause** then **Resume**.
- If the issue persists, leave and rejoin the consultation.
- Keep the patient's phone number handy as a backup.

**Result**: the consultation resumes after resolving the technical issue.
